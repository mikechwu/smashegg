# Guandan (掼蛋): consequences of failing a level-A attempt — research note

## 1. Documented consequences for A-attempt failure

Across Chinese-language rule pages and pagat.com, the consensus mechanic is:

- **Any single loss at level A** (score 1-4, or worse, at Level A): the team stays *at* level A numerically, but loses its "declarer" status — it becomes the opponent team next hand, and the next hand is played at whatever level the new declarers (the winners) are promoted to. This is not really a "variant" — it's how normal-play works in every Sheng-Ji-style climbing game: the hand's level is always set by whoever is declarer. Source: pagat.com — "If the declarers lose on level A, they become the opponents and the winners become declarers and are promoted as usual." (https://www.pagat.com/climbing/guan_dan.html)
- **Three failed A-attempts (not necessarily consecutive)**: the team is demoted all the way back to level 2, and its failure counter resets. This is the most widely documented "hard" consequence, appearing in essentially every source found:
  - pagat.com: "If a team have been declarers on level A three times ... without winning, after the third unsuccessful attempt they are demoted back to the lowest level – level 2." (https://www.pagat.com/climbing/guan_dan.html)
  - 联众世界掼蛋游戏介绍: "三次不过A会根据房间要求来进行处理（继续打A不会降级；降级从2重新打）" — i.e., platform/room setting decides: either continue attempting A with no demotion, or demote to 2. (https://www.ourgame.com/game/game-intro-new/df/sc/h2g/egglaying_002.html)
  - 机关职工掼牌比赛规则 (a formal tournament rule PDF): "三次打A 不过时回到2" (demote to 2 after 3 failed A attempts). (https://www.cup.edu.cn/jiguan/docs/2024-03/a37a68b7f82c405f94a214c2becf7cc3.pdf)
  - 掼蛋比赛规则 zhihu discussion confirms the "3 failed attempts → back to 2" framing is the commonly cited rule, though posters note ambiguity in how the count is reset. (https://www.zhihu.com/question/642304224)
- **"Stay at A forever, keep re-attempting, never demoted"**: explicitly documented as a real, named alternative — the 联众 (Ourgame) platform text above states some rooms configure "继续打A不会降级" (continuing to attempt A without demotion) as a room-configurable option, and pagat notes "Many groups do not use the rule that a team on level Ace that loses to a play consisting entirely of Aces are demoted to level Two" (i.e., some drop even the special all-Aces-instant-demotion rule).
- **Special instant-demotion sub-rule**: if the declarers lose at A and the opponent's winning final play consists entirely of Aces, the declaring team is demoted to level 2 immediately (bypassing the 3-strikes counter). (pagat.com)
- **Named regional variant — demote to K instead of 2**: "Jared McComb reports ... Many play that a team that are declarers on level A for three consecutive deals without winning the game are demoted only to King, not all the way down to Two." (pagat.com)
- **Named regional variant — demote to opponents' level (David Wu)**: "the unsuccessful declarers would be demoted to a level equal to the opponents' level at the beginning of that deal. For example if the score was A-9 ... the new score would be 9-J." (pagat.com) — This is the closest documented analogue to a "play at opponents' level" idea, but it is framed as a demotion event after the 3rd failure, applied once, not as an ongoing per-hand suspension mechanic.

## 2. Does the "suspend-and-play-at-opponents'-level, never demoted, resume attempting A on next win" pattern appear anywhere?

**Partially, but not as a distinct named variant — it's arguably just how the core mechanic already works for single failures, and no source describes an *unlimited*/permanent version of it.**

- For a *single* A-attempt failure (not the 3rd), what pagat.com describes is functionally identical to what you're describing: the team keeps level A, is not demoted, becomes the "opponent" side, and subsequent hands are played at whatever level the current declarers (former opponents) hold — until the A-team wins a hand and gets to attempt A again as declarer. This is standard/core behavior, not a labeled regional variant, and it isn't described as being permanently sustained — it's just business-as-usual for one or two failures before the 3-strikes rule kicks in.
- No source found (across the ~9 pages/searches consulted, including pagat.com's "Variants" section, Chinese sources, Wikipedia zh, and BoardGameGeek) describes a rule where a team can **fail unlimited times** at A, never be demoted, and simply keep cycling through opponents'-level hands indefinitely until winning — every documented rule set caps this with a 3-failed-attempts trigger (to level 2, to K, or to the opponents' level per David Wu's variant), or leaves it as a room-configurable toggle between "demote" and "keep re-attempting" (Ourgame/联众), but the latter is not combined with a "play at opponents' level" description — it says "继续打A" (keep attempting A), not "revert to opponent-level play."
- **This is a clean null result** for the specific unlimited/never-demoted + opponents'-level-suspension combination as a documented, named pattern. It is closest to (a) the ordinary single-failure mechanic (not unlimited, not named), and (b) the Ourgame "continue attempting A, no demotion" toggle (unlimited, but does not describe reverting to opponents'-level play — it implies you keep trying A every hand).

## 3. Suggested tag

**VARIANT** (with a **CORE**-adjacent caveat) — the single-failure "stay at A, become opponent, next hand at opponents' level" behavior is core/standard mechanics found in the majority of sources; the "no demotion ever, indefinitely" toggle is a documented but unnamed room/platform variant (Ourgame); the specific claim of an *unlimited, indefinitely sustained* suspend-and-resume loop with no eventual demotion trigger is **UNCERTAIN / NOT FOUND** as its own documented, named ruleset — treat as a plausible extrapolation rather than an attested variant.
